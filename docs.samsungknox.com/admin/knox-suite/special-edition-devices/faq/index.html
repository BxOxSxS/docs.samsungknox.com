Special edition devices FAQ

Last updated October 2nd, 2024

On this page, you’ll find answers to the most common questions that new customers have about special edition devices.

Special edition devices are exclusive bundles of devices and Knox license keys, sold by select US resellers. The following two special edition device bundles are available for US customers:

  • Verizon Enterprise Edition
  • Galaxy Healthcare Edition

The special edition device bundles differ from Enterprise Edition device bundles in device model and license types offered. For a comparative overview, see the following table.

Device model License type
Verizon Enterprise Edition Samsung Galaxy S24 Three-year Knox Suite license
Galaxy Healthcare Edition Samsung Galaxy A15 Two-year Knox E-FOTA license and a two-year Knox Configure Dynamic Edition license
Enterprise Edition Contact your local reseller to find out what Enterprise Edition devices are available in your region One-year Knox Suite license

If you’re experiencing issues or if you have additional questions, you can submit a support ticket to get help from our team. To submit a support ticket:

  1. Sign in to Samsungknox.com using your Samsung account.
  2. In the top-right corner, click the question mark next to your profile icon.
  3. Click Submit/View support ticket to request access to the support ticketing system.

Contact your local Knox reseller to renew your existing license.

When a device reseller uploads additional devices to your account, you have the option to either generate a new license key or append the devices to an already existing license key.

When you choose to append, the existing licenses expiration date will be modified to ensure that all the seats have the same expiration date. That way, the original seats won’t expire before the new seats on the same license key.

The modified expiration date is calculated as the average of all the expiration dates of the seats on the license, which can either reduce or extend your existing license expiration date.

For example, if your existing license key currently has 2 seats and expires on November 22, 2024, and you’d like to append an additional five seats with an expiry date on January 3, 2025, your new license key will have seven seats and a modified expiry date of December 22, 2024.
