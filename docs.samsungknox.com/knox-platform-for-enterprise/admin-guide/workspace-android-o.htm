Use Workspace (Android O)

On this page

This section provides information for using Knox Workspace and modifying settings to streamline your deployments.

NOTE – The following instructions are for Workspace running on Android O. See the following pages for Workspace in Android P.

Log-in to Workspace

Once you create the Workspace you can log into it at any time to access your secure space and protected files. There are two different views you can open the Workspace container in, Folder view or Launcher View. When you open your Workspace, it will lock automatically after 10 minutes and will need re-authentication to access the files. You can change the default authentication time out if needed.

To log into the

  1. Tap the Workspace icon on your apps screen.
  2. Enter the unlock method you selected when you set up the Workspace.
  3. The Workspace screen is displayed.

Or:

  1. Pull down the notification panel.
  2. Tap Switch to Workspace mode. The Knox home screen is displayed.
    • Once inside the container, if you tap Switch to Personal mode, the Knox container does not automatically lock and can be opened without authentication. It will automatically lock according to the lock out time period set in Workspace Settings.

If you don't see the above drop down notification, you have to enable this feature in Workspace Settings.

  1. While in the container, tap Workspace settings.
  2. Tap Notifications and data.
  3. Enable Quick mode change.

Exit Workspace 

Once you exit the Workspace, it will stay unlocked for the default authentication time out session set in the Workspace Settings.

If you don't manually lock the Workspace before the time-out session has passed, you will still be able to re-open running apps without authentication from the app drawer.
  • Folder View
  • Launcher View

To return to the personal space, either:

  • Tap the Home Button. or back button. This method does not immediately lock your Workspace. It will lock after your defined lock time-out period has passed.
  • Inside the Knox container, Tap the Settings Menu  > Lock. This method immediately locks Workspace.

To return to the personal space, either:

  1. Tap the Personal icon in the lower left corner of the (Undefined variable: Variables.Workspace) home screen, or
  2. Swipe down from the top to show the Notifications bar; then tap Switch to Personal mode.

Unenroll Workspace

You can unenroll / un-install your device from your enterprise’s MDM system. Once you unenroll, your device MDM policies will be removed , your Workspcae will be deleted, and any data within the container will be permanently erased. You can unenroll using the following methods:

Reset forgotten unlock method

If you don’t remember your unlock code, you can reset your it though your MDM. For security reasons, there is no way to reset the password on the device or inside the Workspace without an MDM.

Change unlock method

Changing the Workspace unlock method allows you to choose between PIN, password, pattern or fingerprint.  You can disable certain unlock methods if desired through your MDM. In this case, the unlock options will be greyed out. You can also require a strong password with a combination of uppercase and lowercase characters, numbers, and special symbols, or two-factor authentication. You can set up a biometric fingerprint password to access the secure Knox container, and select either a PIN, password, or pattern as the second authentication method.

To Change the Workspace unlock method:

  1. Open Workspace.
  2. Tap the Settings menu > Workspace Settings > Lock and security > Workspace Lock Type
  3. Follow the on screen instructions to change your unlock method: PIN, password, pattern or fingerprint.

Change layout

Workspace offers two layouts to choose from: Folder and Launcher. Depending on your MDM settings, the default setting may differ upon initial launch.

  • Folder View: This offers quick access to your Workspace protected apps in a single folder when the Workspace symbol is tapped.  Workspace protected apps have a blue lock icon in the bottom right corner. The separation between Workspace mode and the personal mode is less obvious in Folder View than Launcher view. This view is useful for those who need to access Workspace apps periodically on the go, but still want quicker access to personal apps.
  • Launcher View: This changes your Android phone to an entire new "Workspace only" launcher when the Workspace symbol is tapped. Only Workspace apps are displayed here, and you cannot access most personal apps until you return to the personal side. The separation between Workspace mode and Personal mode is more obvious. This view is useful for those who like to feel like they are using 2 phones, at separate times during the day. For example: at work then at home.
  • Switch to Folder View
  • Switch to Launcher View

To change the Knox layout:

  1. Open the Workspace.
  2. Tap Workspace Settings
  3. Tap Workspace Style.
  4. Select Folder.

To change the Knox layout:

  1. Open the Workspace.
  2. Tap Workspace Settings.
  3. Tap Workspace Style.
  4. Select Launcher

Install apps

You can install apps into your Workspace container with Google Play. This can be controlled by an IT policy. By default, Google Play is enabled. If you don't see the Google Play Icon in side the container, then you can't add apps into the container. Check your IT policies if this is the case.

Delete apps

You can delete apps from your Workspace if you are no longer using them. This will remove all data and personal settings from the app. Deleting apps can be controlled with an MDM policy.

  • Folder View
  • Launcher View

To delete apps from the Workspace:

  1. In the Workspace, tap the menu bar.
  2. Tap Delete Apps.
  3. Select the app to delete.

To delete apps from the Workspace:

  1. In the Workspace, tap and hold the app to remove.
  2. Select disable or uninstall.
    • If you don't see these options, you can't remove the app by default.

Move data in and out

Some data can be moved in and out of the . This can be controlled by an IT policy.

To move data in and out of the Workspace:

  1. Highlight or check the desired file.
  2. Tap the Settings menu
  3. If the file can be moved, Move to Workspaceor Move to Personal Mode is displayed.

Workspace shortcuts in personal mode

Direct shortcuts to apps in Workspace can be added in your personal space.These direct shortcuts allow you to open a Workspace app without having to navigate to the Workspace Folder or Launcher menu. None of the security benefits of the container are lost by using Workspace shortcuts in your personal mode – If a shortcut is created, Workspace will still prompt for authentication before allowing access.

To ensure you are always using the secure Workspace version of an app in your personal space, look for the blue badge icon in the bottom right corner.

To create Workspace shortcuts in personal mode, you must turn off the Hide Workspace apps setting

  1. In the Workspace, tap Workspace settings.
  2. Tap Workspace Style
  3. Turn Hide Workspace apps on or off.

Change Workspace session timeout

The Workspace session timeout is the length of time that must pass before the container will lock itself and require re-authentication to access it's files. By default, the timeout is ten minutes. If you don't do anything in the Workspace for this length of time, you will need to re-enter the unlock method. There are a few options to choose from: when the screen turns off, when the device restarts, or after 5, 10 or 30 minutes.

To change the Workspace session timeout:

  1. In the Workspace, tap Workspace Settings.
  2. Tap Auto lock Workspace
  3. Select the new timeout period.

Modify caller ID display information

Yu can configure caller ID to display information about your Workspace contacts when in personal mode. By default, Workspace contacts information will be displayed in the caller ID of the personal space when you receive an incoming call.

To modify the caller ID.

  1. In the Workspace, tap Workspace Settings.
  2. Tap Notifications and Data.
  3. Turn Show caller IDs on or off.

Chamber

The Chamber is a folder inside the Workspace that is secured with defense-grade Sensitive Data Protection (SDP) technology. Data remains encrypted even after the user has exited the Workspace or has turned off the device.

You can use Chamber the same way you would use any other folder inside the container. You can create folders within Chamber and move files in and out of Chamber as necessary.

  1. In the Workspace, open the App drawer and tap My Files
  2. Navigate to the file that you want to move.
  3. Tap and hold the file to select it.
  4. Tap More options ⋮ > Move.
  5. Select Internal Storage > Chamber.
  6. Tap Move Here.
